Beginner's Cutting Guide

All About Cutting

Cutting is a fitness phase focused on reducing body fat while preserving lean muscle mass. It’s the opposite of bulking, where the goal is to build muscle and size. Whether you're coming off a bulking phase or want to get leaner and more defined, cutting is a structured approach to fat loss without sacrificing muscle.

What Is Cutting?

Cutting means eating in a calorie deficit—consuming fewer calories than your body needs to maintain weight. This forces the body to use stored fat for energy, leading to fat loss. The goal is to lose fat without losing muscle, which requires a balance of proper nutrition, exercise, and recovery.

Types of Cutting

Lean Cutting

  • Focuses on a moderate calorie deficit
  • Prioritizes muscle preservation
  • Typically involves high protein intake
  • Ideal for beginners and intermediate lifters

Aggressive Cutting

  • Involves a larger calorie deficit for faster fat loss
  • Can result in faster fat loss, but may also lead to muscle loss
  • Often used by experienced lifters looking for quick results

How Many Calories Should You Eat?

To cut, you’ll need to eat fewer calories than your maintenance level. Start with a moderate calorie deficit of 250–500 calories per day to lose fat without sacrificing muscle.

  • Maintenance calories: The number of calories you need to maintain your current weight
  • TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure): A calculator can help estimate your maintenance
  • Start with a 250-calorie deficit and adjust based on progress

Macronutrient Breakdown

Here’s a general guide for cutting macros:

  • Protein: 1–1.2g per pound → Preserves muscle during fat loss
  • Carbs: 1.5–2g per pound → Provides energy for workouts
  • Fats: 0.3–0.4g per pound → Supports hormone function

🏋 Training During a Cut

When cutting, maintaining muscle mass is key. Your workouts should focus on strength training to preserve muscle and cardio to burn fat.

  • Strength training: 3–5x per week using compound and isolation lifts
  • Cardio: 2–4x per week using HIIT or steady-state cardio
  • Goal: Maintain muscle while promoting fat loss

😴 Recovery & Sleep

Fat loss happens both during workouts and recovery, so ensure you prioritize rest.

  • SleepAim for 7–9 hours per night to support fat loss and muscle recovery
  • Rest days:Take at least 1–2 days off per week to avoid overtraining
  • Avoid under-eating:Getting too few calories can slow down your metabolism and lead to muscle loss

What to Expect During Cutting

  • Expect a fat loss of 0.5–1 pound per week
  • Some muscle loss may occur-minimize it with strength training and protein
  • Track progress through photos, body measurements, and strength levels

⏳ How Long Should You cut?

  • Cutting phases last 6–12 weeks on average
  • Choose duration based on your goals
  • Transition to maintenance once you’ve reached your desired leanness

✅ Cutting Tips for Beginners

  • Plan your meals ahead of time
  • Keep protein intake high to protect muscle
  • Be consistent—results take time
  • Track your calories to stay accountable
  • Limit excessive cardio
  • Don't stay in a deficit forever—have an end goal
